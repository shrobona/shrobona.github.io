TLDR; 
- 👋 Hi, I’m Shrobona Karkun-Sen
- 👀 I’m interested in all things spatial about cities in India and the US, urban infrastructure, and land systems.

**Longer introduction:**

I am an Assistant Teaching Professor at the University of Wisconsin-La Crosse since Fall 2024. I teach courses on critical cartography and human geography. 
I have worn multiple hats in and out of academia. I was trained as an architect in India and worked as a Senior Architect before discovering my passion for understanding various facets of cities using geospatial methods. This led to a Master's in Urban Spatial Analytics (MUSA) at the University of Pennsylvania, later followed by a two-year stint working on the land vacancy problem at the Philadelphia Land Bank. Through my doctorate in Geography and Urban Studies at Temple University, I combined my mixed-methods geospatial training to understand rapid urbanization in Delhi-NCT and its peripheries. 
In 2024, I initiated the Spatial Analysis of Regional and Urban Systems (SARUS) group at the Department of Geography and Environmental Science. To learn more about SARUS, please visit the group website.
